About Sea Turtles

Learn more about the incredible sea turtles and their importance in marine ecosystems.

What Are Sea Turtles?

Sea turtles are marine reptiles that have adapted to life in the ocean. They are known for their large, protective shells and long migrations across the seas.

Characteristics of Sea Turtles

  • Sea turtles have a hard shell called a "carapace" that protects them from predators.
  • They have flipper-like limbs that help them swim long distances in the ocean.
  • Unlike land turtles, sea turtles cannot retract their heads into their shells.
  • They breathe air but can hold their breath for extended periods while underwater.

Sea Turtle Species

There are seven species of sea turtles, and each has unique traits:

  • Green Sea Turtle: Known for its greenish-colored fat and large size.
  • Leatherback Sea Turtle: The largest species, known for its leathery shell.
  • Loggerhead Sea Turtle: Named for its large head and powerful jaws.
  • Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle: One of the smallest and most endangered species.

Why Are Sea Turtles Important?

Sea turtles play a vital role in marine ecosystems:

  • They help maintain the balance of marine food chains by eating jellyfish and other marine life.
  • Sea turtles contribute to the health of seagrass beds by grazing on the vegetation, which promotes the growth of new plants.
  • Their nesting behavior helps in the circulation of nutrients in beach ecosystems.
