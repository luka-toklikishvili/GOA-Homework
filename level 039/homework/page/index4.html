Introduction

Bacteria are microscopic, single-celled organisms that exist in a variety of environments. They can be found in soil, water, air, and even in the human body.

Types of Bacteria

  • Cocci (Spherical)
  • Bacilli (Rod-shaped)
  • Spirilla (Spiral-shaped)
  • Vibrio (Comma-shaped)

Functions of Bacteria

Bacteria play several essential roles in nature, such as decomposing organic material, fixing nitrogen, and aiding in digestion in humans and animals.

Importance of Bacteria

While some bacteria can cause diseases, the majority are beneficial. They are crucial for ecological balance, nutrient cycling, and the production of antibiotics.
