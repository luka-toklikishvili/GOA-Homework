History of McDonald's

McDonald's was founded in 1940 by Richard and Maurice McDonald in San Bernardino, California. Initially, it was a drive-in restaurant serving hamburgers, fries, and milkshakes. In 1954, Ray Kroc, a businessman, partnered with the McDonald brothers to expand the brand. By 1955, Kroc had opened the first McDonald's franchise in Des Plaines, Illinois, which marked the beginning of McDonald's global expansion.

Today, McDonald's operates over 40,000 locations worldwide, making it the largest fast-food chain in terms of revenue and number of locations.

McDonald's Menu

McDonald's menu is known for its consistency across the globe, with some variations based on local tastes. Here are some of the most iconic items:

  • Big Mac: The iconic burger with two all-beef patties, special sauce, lettuce, cheese, pickles, and onions on a sesame seed bun.
  • Quarter Pounder: A larger beef patty served with cheese, pickles, onions, mustard, and ketchup.
  • Chicken McNuggets: Breaded and deep-fried chicken nuggets served with dipping sauces.
  • McFlurry: A dessert made with vanilla soft serve ice cream and a variety of mix-ins like Oreo, M&M's, and more.
  • World Famous Fries: McDonald's golden, crispy fries that are beloved worldwide.
  • McCafe: A range of coffee and espresso drinks, including lattes, cappuccinos, and iced coffees.

McDonald's is also known for offering breakfast items like the Egg McMuffin, Sausage McGriddles, and hotcakes.

Fun Facts About McDonald's

  • McDonald's serves over 69 million customers daily across more than 100 countries.
  • The Golden Arches are one of the most recognizable logos in the world.
  • The largest McDonald's in the world is located in Beijing, China.
  • McDonald's owns the world's largest toy distribution network, with millions of toys included in Happy Meals each year.
  • The longest-serving McDonald's franchise is located in Downey, California, which has been operating since 1953.
  • McDonald's has its own trademarked "special sauce" used in the Big Mac.
